On the bottom row, one can see the even numbers from 12 to 4 (or 4 to 12, reading in the other direction). Looking for other sequences in the grid, one can find the triangular numbers from 1 to 15. After further examination, one can find four other sequencesof numbers, with a term in the middle changed. This suggests that the even numbers from 2 to 12 had the first term changed, and the triangular numbers from 1 to 15 had the last term changed. In the below grid, the sequences are colored in different shades of green and the changed terms are highlighted in different shades of red:

20 15 99 64 12 10 8 6 4 6

The sequences are as follows:

  • 6 (replacing 2), 4, 6, 8, 10, 12 (even numbers)
  • 1, 9 (replacing 1), 2, 3, 5, 8 (Fibonacci numbers)
  • 2, 4, 23 (replacing 8), 16, 32, 64 (powers of 2)
  • 2, 3, 5, 23 (replacing 7), 11, 13 (primes)
  • 1, 4, 9, 16, 48 (replacing 25), 36 (squares)
  • 1, 3, 6, 10, 15, 63 (replacing 21) (triangular numbers)

The order of the sequences by position of the different term is the same as the order alphabetically by name. Taking the different terms in that order, we get 6, 9, 23, 23, 48, 63, which is useless. However, all of the different terms are bigger than the corresponding expected terms. Taking the difference between the different terms and the expected terms, we get 4, 8, 15, 16, 23, 42, which upon being searched turns out to be The Numbers from Lost. Given the four-letter enumeration of the answer, it's now clear what the answer is.

Answer: lost
