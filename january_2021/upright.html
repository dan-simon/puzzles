Divide the below grid into ten rectangles. Each grid cell is in exactly one rectangle. The number in a numbered grid cell is the sum of the number of cells in its rectangle above it, the number of cells in its rectangle directly to its right, and one for it itself. For example, a numbered cell at the bottom left of a 6x5 rectangle (6 high, 5 across) would have the number 5 (5 above) + 4 (4 to the right) + 1 (itself) = 10. A numbered cell in the middle of an 11x9 rectangle would also have the number 10. All but one rectangle contains exactly one number (the remaining rectangle has no number).
